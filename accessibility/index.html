Universal Design

The Web Accessibility Initiative, or WAI, is the foundation of making content accessible on the Web. Let's take a look at its main points!

Semantic Elements

Let's say you've got content you want to present like this:

The Dog Site - Your Home for Dog Information
Home - Varieties - Other Info
The Varities of Dogs
There are at least three different kinds of dogs - let's read about them.
Dog Types
• Large
• Small
• Other

[... more content ...]

It's not particularly helpful if it's not organized. Stripped of any visual styling like spacing, font weight, italicization, and font size, it'll read like this:

The Dog Site - Your Home for Dog Information Home - Varieties - Other Info The Varities of Dogs There are at least three different kinds of dogs - let's read about them. Dog Types • Large • Small • Other [... more content ...]

That's already jumbled together and confusing, and it'd be even worse if it was longer. Regions (source) can help differentiate content, both visually and for assistive technologies.

Headers

The first section looks like a title and navigation - content that will be repeated across different pages of the website. (Click to expand the previous content)
The Dog Site - Your Home for Dog Information
Home - Varieties - Other Info

This is fine to skim over visually, but if you need to wade through it on each page, it can get repetitive fast. Navigating through different pages can look like this:

The Dog Site - Your Home for Dog Information Home - Varieties - Other Info Home [...]
The Dog Site - Your Home for Dog Information Home - Varieties - Other Info The Varities of Dogs [...]
The Dog Site - Your Home for Dog Information Home - Varieties - Other Info More Info [...]

The only new content comes after the "Other Info" link, so let's give the user the ability to skip over that repeated content:

<header>
    The Dog Site
    Your Home for Dog Information
    Home - Varieties - Other Info
</header>

Wrapping items in a header element is a start, but we're still treating all the contents of that header the same. Let's make the roles of each section of text a little clearer!
